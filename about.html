About Mithila

Mithila is a cultural and historical region in the Indian subcontinent, renowned for its rich heritage, art, language, and traditions. Spanning parts of Bihar, India, and the eastern Terai of Nepal, Mithila is the land of Goddess Sita and the birthplace of Maithili culture.

The Heart of Mithila

Mithila is not just a place — it is an emotion, a living tradition, and a cradle of ancient Indian civilization.

The region derives its name from King Mithi, who founded the ancient kingdom of Videha. It is mentioned in the Ramayana and the Mahabharata and is revered as the birthplace of Goddess Sita, the consort of Lord Rama.

Today, Mithila is a vibrant cultural hub known for its unique Madhubani art, the melodious Maithili language, exquisite temples, and colourful festivals. The people of Mithila have preserved their heritage for centuries while embracing the modern world.

This digital portal is an ode to Mithila — a platform to share its stories, art, and culture with the world.
